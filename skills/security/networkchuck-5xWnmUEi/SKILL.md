## Road Map to Get Started in Cyber Security

Welcome! This roadmap is designed to help you land your dream job as a cybersecurity professional, specifically focusing on ethical hacking. It's been two years since I last made a video on this topic, and things have changed, but the core principles remain the same.

### What Hasn't Changed
- **The Road Map**: The roadmap remains focused on getting you a job quickly and landing your dream job within six figures.
- **No Gimmicks or Shortcuts**: Success comes from hard work on a clear path without shortcuts or gimmicks.
- **No College Requirement**: You don’t need a college degree to start.

### Phase Zero: Applying for Jobs
- **Start Applying Now**: Don't wait for the perfect job. Apply for entry-level help desk or technical support roles right away.
- **Why Help Desk Roles?**:
  - These are entry-level positions that can provide valuable experience and skills.
  - They offer a path to learning more advanced cybersecurity concepts.

### Phase One: Building Your Foundation
1. **Customer Service Skills**: Focus on developing excellent customer service skills. This is crucial for any IT role.
2. **Basic IT Experience**: Gain basic IT experience through internships, part-time jobs, or personal projects.
3. **Portfolio Development**: Build a portfolio of projects that demonstrate your problem-solving and technical abilities.

### Phase Two: Learning Cyber Security
1. **Online Courses**: Enroll in online courses to learn the basics of cybersecurity:
   - **Cybrary**
   - **Coursera**
   - **Udemy**
2. **Certifications**: Consider obtaining certifications like CompTIA A+, CompTIA Network+, and CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker).
3. **Reading Material**: Read books and articles on cybersecurity:
   - "The Web Application Hacker's Handbook" by Dafydd Stuttard
   - "Penetration Testing: A Hands-On Guide" by Marcus Pinto

### Phase Three: Practical Experience
1. **Capture The Flag (CTF) Competitions**: Participate in CTFs to gain hands-on experience and improve your hacking skills.
2. **Penetration Testing**: Perform penetration testing on ethical targets to practice real-world scenarios.
3. **Volunteering**: Offer your services as a volunteer for cybersecurity events or non-profits.

### Phase Four: Networking
1. **Join Cybersecurity Communities**: Join online communities like Reddit’s r/netsec, Twitter groups, and local meetups.
2. **Attend Conferences**: Attend cybersecurity conferences and workshops to network with professionals and learn from experts.
3. **Mentorship**: Seek out mentors in the field who can provide guidance and support.

### Phase Five: Job Search
1. **Update Your Resume**: Tailor your resume for each job application, focusing on relevant IT experience and skills.
2. **Networking**: Use your network to find job opportunities that may not be advertised publicly.
3. **Interview Preparation**: Prepare for interviews by practicing common cybersecurity questions and preparing case studies.

### Phase Six: Continuous Learning
1. **Stay Updated**: Stay informed about the latest trends, tools, and techniques in cybersecurity.
2. **Advanced Courses**: Consider advanced courses to deepen your knowledge:
   - "Cybersecurity Essentials" by Cybrary
   - "Penetration Testing with Kali Linux" by Offensive Security
3. **Certifications**: Pursue more advanced certifications like CEH, OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional), or GCFA (Global Certified Forensics Analyst).

### Conclusion
This roadmap is designed to help you build a strong foundation in cybersecurity and land your dream job. Remember, persistence and hard work are key. Good luck!
