# Guide to Internal Network Security

## Step 1: Understanding Internal Network Security
- Internal network security differs from perimeter security as it deals with vulnerabilities within the network after an attacker gains access.
  
## Step 2: The Concept of Configuration Debt
- Recognize that configuration debt arises when security configurations are neglected in favor of compatibility with older systems. 

## Step 3: Analyzing Defense in Depth
- Assess your defense in depth strategies; check if adequate multi-layered security controls are present within the internal network.

## Step 4: Identify Common Misconfigurations
- Focus on common misconfigurations, especially in Windows Active Directory environments, that could lead to vulnerabilities.

## Step 5: Audit Internal Networks
- Regularly audit internal networks to discover misconfigurations, utilizing tools and methodologies designed for internal network exploitation.

## Step 6: Continuous Monitoring
- Implement continuous monitoring processes to identify configuration changes and vulnerabilities as they arise.

## Step 7: Educate Your Team
- Train your security team on the nuances of internal network security versus perimeter defense, emphasizing the importance of addressing configuration debt.

## Step 8: Review and Update Policies Regularly
- Regularly review and update security policies to ensure alignment with current threats and vulnerabilities that have been identified in your audits.

## Step 9: Collaborate with Other Teams
- Foster collaboration between security, IT, and network teams to ensure that security-conscious practices are integrated into network operations.

## Step 10: Leverage External Expertise
- When necessary, engage external security experts or consultancies to perform thorough assessments and provide insights into best practices for internal network security.
