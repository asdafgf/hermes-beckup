### Guide to Navigating Cisco Certification Changes

Cisco has made significant changes to all their certifications, impacting those who are currently studying or planning to pursue them. Here’s a guide to help you navigate these changes effectively:

#### 1. Understanding the Changes:
   - **Complete Overhaul**: Cisco has updated its entire certification portfolio, not just individual certifications like the CCNA or CCNP.
   - **Adaptation to Technology**: The updates aim to reflect the rapid technological advancements, ensuring that your skills align with current industry needs.

#### 2. Key Changes:
   - **Single Pathway**: The previous multiple tracks for Cisco certifications (like CCNA security, CCNA collaboration, etc.) are consolidated into a single CCNA certification.
   - **Discontinuation of Legacy Certifications**: Certifications like CCENT and others have been retired. Starting in February 2020, testing on these old frameworks will cease.
   
#### 3. What It Means for You:
   - **Study Continuity**: If you are studying for an older certification, assess whether you need to adjust your study plan according to the new certification requirements.
   - **Focus Areas**: The new certifications include additional focus areas such as ACI programmability and SD-WAN, among others. Familiarize yourself with these topics as they are part of the new learning paths.

#### 4. Preparing for the New Certifications:
   - **Stay Informed**: Regularly check Cisco's official announcements and resources for the latest updates on certification changes.
   - **Study Resources**: Utilize updated study materials, practice exams, and training courses that align with the new certification structure.
   - **Engage with the Community**: Connect with peers and professionals who are also navigating these changes. Join forums, attend webinars, and participate in study groups.

#### 5. Conclusion:
The transition to the new certification structure is significant, but understanding the changes and adapting your study approach will ensure you remain on track for your career development in networking. Don’t hesitate to seek out resources and community support as you embark on this updated certification journey.
