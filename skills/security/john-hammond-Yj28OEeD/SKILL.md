**name:** ransomware-negotiation

**description:** A step-by-step guide on how to negotiate with ransomware operators.

**body:**
1. **Identify the Ransom Note**: Once your system is locked and encrypted, look for the ransom note typically found in a text file or on your desktop. This note will contain instructions from the ransomware operators.

2. **Do Not Pay the Ransom**: The first step should always be to avoid paying the ransom. Cyber criminals often demand payment in exchange for decrypting your files, but there is no guarantee that you will get your data back even after paying.

3. **Contact Authorities**: Report the incident to local law enforcement agencies such as the FBI or your country’s equivalent. They can provide guidance and support in dealing with ransomware attacks.

4. **Seek Professional Help**: Engage cybersecurity experts who specialize in ransomware recovery. These professionals may have tools and techniques that can help decrypt your files without paying the ransom.

5. **Review Chat Logs**: If possible, access any chat logs or communication records between you and the ransomware operators. This can provide insights into their demands and behavior during negotiations.

6. **Negotiate if Necessary**: While it’s generally not recommended to negotiate with ransomware operators, in some cases, you might consider doing so as a last resort. Be prepared for haggling and be aware that there is no guarantee of success.

7. **Document Everything**: Keep detailed records of all communications, including dates, times, and any messages exchanged. This documentation can be useful if law enforcement becomes involved.

8. **Backup Important Data**: If you are unable to recover your data through negotiation or professional help, consider backing up important files from a clean system or an external backup.
