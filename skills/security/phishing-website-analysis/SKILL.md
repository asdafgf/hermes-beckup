  ## Introduction

  This guide will walk you through the analysis of a phishing website that led to a Twitter account takeover. The incident occurred on August 11th, 2024, and was reported by Luke from their team.

  ## Malicious Link Analysis

  The malicious link included in the fishing email directed users to a URL that was tracked using Srid, a scalable email delivery service. This click tracking link would redirect victims to a fake spear phishing website for credential harvesting.

  ## Email Comparison

  A side-by-side comparison of the fake fishing email and the actual notification from Twitter is provided. The fake email lacked correct logo placement and missing words, while the real email included additional instructions such as suggestions to change your password and review apps.

  ## Phishing Website Analysis

  The phishing website itself was analyzed for its technical merits. It was designed to mimic a legitimate login page, raising alarm bells in potential victims and leading them to click the malicious link.

  ## Conclusion

  This guide provides a detailed analysis of the phishing incident, highlighting the importance of recognizing such tactics and taking appropriate security measures.
