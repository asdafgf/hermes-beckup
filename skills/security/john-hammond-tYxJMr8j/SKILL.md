# name: active-directory-certificate-services

# description: A summary of vulnerabilities and configurations in Active Directory Certificate Services, highlighting common misconfigurations and their risks.

# body: 
This guide provides an overview of the vulnerabilities and configurations related to Active Directory Certificate Services (AD CS), one of the most commonly misconfigured services in corporate environments. Throughout this session, we will cover the following topics:

1. **Introduction to Active Directory Certificate Services**: An overview of what AD CS is and its importance in managing digital certificates within Windows environments.

2. **Vulnerabilities in AD CS**: Discussing the various vulnerabilities associated with AD CS, as highlighted by recent research from Spectre Ops. These vulnerabilities have contributed to systemic misconfigurations over the past decades.

3. **Common Misconfigurations**: Identifying typical misconfigurations that organizations face and their implications for security.

4. **Asymmetric Encryption Basics**: A brief overview of asymmetric encryption, discussing public and private keys and their roles in secure communications.

5. **Key Exchange Mechanisms**: Understanding how key exchanges are typically performed and the importance of verifying the authenticity of public keys.

6. **Root of Trust**: Introduction to the concept of a root of trust, which is crucial for establishing secure communications between parties.

7. **Practical Demos**: Live demonstrations illustrating the discussed vulnerabilities and configurations in a controlled lab environment.

By the end of this skill, you will have a solid understanding of AD CS vulnerabilities, their risks, and how to effectively secure this critical Windows service.
