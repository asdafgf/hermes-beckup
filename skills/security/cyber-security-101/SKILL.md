  # Cyber Security 101

  ## Introduction to Cyber Crime

  When you hear about cyber crime, you usually think of ransomware. However, there's another type of malware that often goes unnoticed: information stealing malware. These threats are designed to collect as much personal and financial data as possible from their targets.

  ## What is Information Stealing Malware?

  Information stealing malware, or info stealers, are malicious programs that aim to gather sensitive information such as passwords, credit card details, bank information, and more. One of the most prolific types of info stealer malware is Redline Steeler.

  ## The Redline Steeler Ecosystem

  Eric Clay, a security researcher at Flare, discusses the distribution and prevalence of info stealers on the dark web. He mentions that about 400,000 new Steeler logs are distributed daily through Telegram channels. Redline Steeler is one of the most common and successful info stealer malware.

  ## The Business Model

  Redline Steeler operates using a business model where vendors set up automated Telegram channels to sell their services. Customers pay a monthly fee in cryptocurrency for access to the command and control infrastructure, panel, and distribution capabilities.

  ## Distribution Channels

  Info stealers like Redline Steeler can be distributed through various channels:
  - Malicious advertisements on social media
  - Emails
  - Crack software sites
  - Downloads from malicious websites

  ## Open Source Alternatives

  In addition to commercial options, there are open-source info stealer malware available. These alternatives allow individuals and groups to build their own stealers based on existing code.

  ## Conclusion

  Understanding the nature and distribution of cyber threats is crucial for protecting against them. By staying informed about the latest trends and techniques used by hackers, you can better safeguard your personal and financial information.
