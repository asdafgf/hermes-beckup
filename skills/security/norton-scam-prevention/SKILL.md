  ## Guide to Preventing Norton Scam Emails

  ### Introduction
  Cybersecurity threats are constantly evolving, and one such threat is the Norton scam. This guide aims to help you identify and prevent potential Norton scam emails.

  ### Identifying a Potential Norton Scam Email
  - **Suspicious Content**: Look for emails that promise extended Norton membership or any other benefits without requiring payment.
  - **Unusual Attachments**: Be wary of attachments in unexpected emails, especially PDF files.
  - **Emphasis on Phone Numbers**: If the email emphasizes calling a specific phone number, it's likely a scam.

  ### Example Scenario
  A viewer received an email claiming their Norton membership had been extended and included an invoice for $800. The email contained a suspicious PDF attachment with only a phone number in it.

  ### Steps to Take
  - **Verify the Source**: Check if the sender is legitimate by contacting Norton directly through their official channels.
  - **Do Not Open Attachments**: Do not open any attachments from unknown senders, even if they appear to be from trusted sources.
  - **Check Your Account**: Verify your account status and bank statements for any unauthorized transactions.

  ### Additional Tips
  - **Use Antivirus Software**: Ensure that your antivirus software is up-to-date and running to detect and block potential threats.
  - **Be Cautious with Phone Calls**: If you receive a phone call from someone claiming to be from Norton, hang up immediately and contact Norton directly through their official support channels.

  ### Conclusion
  By following these steps, you can help protect yourself from potential Norton scams. Always remain vigilant and cautious when dealing with unexpected emails or attachments.
